Old Stadium

Originally constructed to coincide with the start of the 1903-04 football season, Ayresome Park was Boro's previous home ground.

Playing in the top division meant Ayrsome Park had attendances of 50,000+ supporters on a regular basis.

Ayresome Park was selected to host 3 competitive matches in the 1966 FIFA World Cup, the year in which England famously won the World Cup.

The Riverside


Technical Specifications Capacity: 34,988
Completion Date: 1995
Build Time: 32 weeks
Build Cost: £16 million

DetailsImages

The Riverside Stadium was built in 1995 as Boro's new home with a capacity of 34,988. During games the Riverside Roar can be heard for miles around the town.

The inaugural game at the Riverside was Boro 2 - 0 Chelsea, played in front of 28,286 fans. Craig Hignett went down in history scoring the first goal at the Riverside, with Jan Age Fjortoft scoring the 2nd.

By the River Side

The Riverside Stadium was erected adjacent to the local river Tees and on the outskirts of Middlesbrough's town centre.

There is good access for supporters visiting Boro's home ground by road and also on foot with many paths and underground tunnels connecting the stadium with the town centre.

Traffic around the grounds access roads, and the main A-roads leading directly to the stadium, can be busy during match days.

Good Times

Boro have experienced many highs in recent years spending 11 consecutive seasons in the Premier League, making it to 3 successive major cup finals culminating in victory in the 2004 Carling Cup.

The most glorious times for Boro were in 2006 when they made it to the 2006 UEFA League Cup Final. After blistering wins in the Qtr-Finals, and then the Semi-Finals, Boro came back from the dead in both matches making history on the way.

Relive the Glory Years and enjoy some of Boro's best moments.
